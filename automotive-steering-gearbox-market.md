<p><p>An automotive steering gearbox is a critical component in a vehicle's steering system, responsible for converting the rotational motion of the steering wheel into the linear motion needed to turn the wheels. It plays a vital role in ensuring precise control and maneuverability, enhancing driver safety and comfort. Steering gearboxes are commonly found in recirculating ball and rack-and-pinion systems, with the latter being more prevalent in modern vehicles due to its compact design and efficiency.</p><p>The Automotive Steering Gearbox Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by advancements in vehicle technology, increasing demand for electric and autonomous vehicles, and stricter safety regulations. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.8% during the forecast period. Key factors contributing to this growth include the rising adoption of electric power steering (EPS) systems, which offer better fuel efficiency and reduced emissions compared to traditional hydraulic systems. Additionally, the integration of advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and the growing preference for lightweight and durable materials are shaping market trends.</p><p>Emerging trends in the market include the development of steer-by-wire systems, which eliminate mechanical linkages for enhanced flexibility and design possibilities. The Asia-Pacific region is anticipated to dominate the market due to rapid urbanization, increasing vehicle production, and rising disposable incomes. Overall, the automotive steering gearbox market is poised for robust growth, driven by technological innovations and evolving consumer demands.</p></p>

<p><p>The **Automotive Steering Gearbox Market** includes two primary types: **Electric Power Steering (EPS) Gearbox** and **Hydraulic Power Steering (HPS) Gearbox**. EPS uses an electric motor to assist steering, offering better fuel efficiency, precision, and adaptability to advanced driver-assistance systems. HPS relies on hydraulic pressure generated by a pump, providing robust steering feel but consuming more energy. EPS dominates modern vehicles due to its efficiency and integration with technology, while HPS remains in older or heavy-duty applications for its durability and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
