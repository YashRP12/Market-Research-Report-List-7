<p><p>Luxury real estate auctions typically occur in two main market types: reserved and unreserved auctions. In a reserved auction, the seller sets a minimum price that must be met for the property to be sold, providing a safety net against undervaluation. Conversely, unreserved auctions have no such minimum, allowing properties to be sold to the highest bidder regardless of the price, often resulting in quick sales but with potentially unpredictable outcomes. Both methods cater to varying seller strategies and market dynamics.</p></p>
