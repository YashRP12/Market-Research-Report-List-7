<p><p>The New Energy Vehicle (NEV) traction motor core market primarily consists of two types: Permanent Magnet Motor Cores and AC Induction Motor Cores. Permanent Magnet Motor Cores utilize magnets to generate a magnetic field, offering high efficiency and torque density, making them suitable for electric vehicles. In contrast, AC Induction Motor Cores rely on electromagnetic induction to operate, typically providing robust performance and lower manufacturing costs, though potentially sacrificing some efficiency compared to permanent magnet designs. Together, they cater to diverse NEV requirements.</p></p>
