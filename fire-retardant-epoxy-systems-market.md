<p><p>The fire retardant epoxy systems market is segmented into several types. Halogenated fire retardants are effective but can produce toxic fumes when burned. Inorganic fire retardants, such as metal hydroxides, are often used for their non-toxic nature and high thermal stability. Organic phosphorus fire retardants offer enhanced durability and effectiveness in preventing combustion. Nitrogenous fire retardants release nitrogen gas when heated, effectively diluting flammable gases. Each type serves distinct applications based on performance, safety, and environmental considerations.</p></p>
