<p><p>Credit card networks operate in various market segments, primarily through interchange fees, assessment fees, and other charges. Interchange fees are paid by merchants' banks to card-issuing banks for processing transactions, serving as compensation for fraud risk. Assessment fees are collected by networks based on transaction volume and facilitate network operations. Other fees can include annual fees, foreign transaction fees, and service charges. Together, these fees comprise the financial framework that supports credit card transactions and rewards programs.</p></p>
