<p><p>Silicon Carbide (SiC) varistors are essential surge protection devices, classified into two main types: tube and disc varistors. Tube varistors are cylindrical in shape, offering robust performance in high-voltage applications, often utilizing their compact design for efficient heat dissipation. Disc varistors, on the other hand, have a flat, circular form, providing versatile mounting options and often being favored for their space-saving attributes. Both types enhance electrical circuit protection, effectively suppressing voltage spikes and ensuring device reliability across various applications.</p></p>
